3. Cookies

Cookies are text information files that your web browser places on your computer when you visit a website. Cookies assist in providing non-personal information from you as an online visitor. It can be used in the customization of your preferences when visiting our website.

Most browsers accept cookies automatically, but can be configured not to accept them or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. If you choose to make your browser refuse cookies, it is possible that some portions of our website will not function optimally when you use them.

However, we do not link the information stored in these cookies directly to any of your personal information you submit while on the website.

We also automatically collect your Internet Protocol (IP) address (which allows us to (a) understand how you use our site and enables us to make changes to our website in order to improve your user experience); and (b)information regarding your computer and your connection to our site (such as your browser type, operating system, and platform etc).

4. Web Beacons

In certain circumstances we also may use Web Beacons to collect anonymous, non-personal information about your use of our website and the sites of selected sponsors and advertisers, and your use of emails, special promotions or newsletters we send to you. 'Web Beacons' are tiny graphic image files imbedded in a web page or email that provide a presence on the web page or email and send back to its home server information from the user’s browser. The information collected by web beacons allows us to statistically monitor how many people are using the website and selected sponsors' and advertisers' sites, or opening our emails, and for what purposes.
